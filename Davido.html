History on Davido

David Adedeji Adeleke (born 21 November 1992), popularly known as Davido, is a Nigerian singer and record producer. He blends traditional African elements with global main stream pop. Davido won the next rated award in 2012 at The Headies after releasing "Dami Duro" , the second single from his debut studio album Omo baba Olowo (2012). Between 2013 and 2015, he released a number of hit singles, and in January 2016, he signed a record deal with sony music. He then founded the record label Davido Music Worldwide (DMW), to which acts Dremo, Mayorkun, Perruzi and Liya have been signed. in July 2016, he released the 5-track EP Son of Mercy, whuch was supported by the singles "Gbade Oshi", "How long" and coolest kid in africa. Davido has been famously referred to as the king of modern-days afrobeats. Davido released, his second studio album A Good Time in November 2019, supported by singles including "Blow My Mind" featuring popular american singer Chris Brown. Davido was citied as one of the top 100 most influential Africans by New African magazine in 2019. He released his third studio album A Better Time, on Friday November 13, 2020. in antocipation tp his birthday (21 November 2021) the afrobeats singer Davido took his instagram story to ask colleagues to each send him a million naira. Davido noted that he recieved N200m from fans and friends and added N50million. He donated N250million to all orphanges across Nigeria. The singer made this known his statement ahead of the birthday. To disburse the money, he constituted a 5-man commitee to take care of the logistics.
